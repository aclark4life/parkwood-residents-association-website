This means that all positions in the Executive Board of the Parkwood Residents Association are open. Positions include two Co-Presidents, a Treasurer, and a Secretary. This is a great opportunity to get involved in your community and make a difference in the issues that are important to you and your neighbors!

Kensington Parkwood ES Modernization

As most Parkwood residents know, Kensington Parkwood Elementary School is a 50-year-old school that badly needs to be renovated and updated. KP was scheduled to be "modernized" during the 2002-2003 school year. However, the County Executive has recommended that the modernization of KP be delayed until
January 2006 due to overall weakness in the economy. The school PTA has been lobbying the County Council and School Board to have the modernization completed as soon as possible. A "new" school, which may include a gym, will be an asset for Parkwood and the entire Kensington community. Parkwood residents are invited to write to the County Council and the School Board if they would like to express support for the modernization. For more information, please contact Dan Shepherdson, at (301) 564-4381.

Submitted by Dan Shepherdson

Debate Over Re-Opening the Gym at the HOC Building

For the past several months the Kensington Gazette has been faithfully reporting the community discussions regarding the gym at the Housing Opportunities Commission (HOC) which is currently housed in the old Kensington Elementary School. Up until 1998, the gym was used for recreational basketball through the Department of Recreation. For a variety of reasons, including perceived under utilization, the gym was closed and is currently being used by the HOC for file storage.

Representatives from several area organizations have been meeting with County Council members to work toward not only re-opening the gym, but also to explore the possibility of housing a part-time community center. This part-time concept would include game tables such as ping-pong, air hockey, etc. and a recreational staff person to serve as a supervisor. Currently the Kensington residents do not have access to a community center facility. Residents that live on Detrick Avenue, across from the HOC, have voiced their opposition to re-opening the gym as it will increase traffic, noise and litter on their block. The March meeting of the PRA will allow for an opportunity for the Parkwood neighborhood to weigh-in on this topic.

Submitted by Liz Brennan

Kensington Residents Form Coalition

In response to mounting frustration regarding quality of life issues, area residents have formed an umbrella organization to work together to address common areas of concern. The Coalition of Kensington Communities (CKC) is a unified body of area civic associations and townships founded to enhance the quality of life in the Kensington community. Towards this end the group will work with local government to address issues of urban design, recreation, transportation, pedestrian accessibility and economic growth affecting greater Kensington.

The CKC held its first meeting in January at the Kensington Firehouse, and PRA was represented by several residents. The following committees were established during this meeting: Traffic, Pedestrian Access/safety, Land Use, Parks and Recreation and Economic Development. It is a goal to have a minimum of representative from each neighhborhood on each committee. If you are interested in becoming involved with the Coalition, please contact Liz Brennan at 301-530-3615. The next meeting of the CKC is Tuesday, March 26th , 7:00 pm at the Firehouse.

Block Captain System to be Resurrected

Want a fun, volunteer job with the Parkwood Residents Association? We are resurrecting our "Block Captain" system. Block Captains will be a very important communication link for our neighborhood. As a Block Captain, you would be responsible for the dissemination of communications from the association to the residents on your block in an situation where there is not enough time to mail a newsletter or when an issue needs a quick response. Block Captains are also needed to report new residents on their block to the Welcome Committee. We need a volunteer to be the Block Captain Director. Please indicate on your survey if you would be willing to serve as a Block Captain or call Valerie Gottron at (301) 493-9002.
